What is bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a revolutionary currency and consensus network that enables a new payment system using digital money. It is the first cryptocurrency or decentralized peer-to-peer payment network that is powered by its users with no central authority or middlemen. From an economist perspective, it is a more perfect and programmable version of fiat money. Fiat money is prone to human mismanagement and inflation by excess money printing, thus depreciating in value over time. Bitcoin’s solves this problem as its supply is limited by code to mint 21 million coins for its existence. Bitcoin is one of the first mathematically created currencies. From a user perspective, bitcoin is pretty much like cash for the Internet.

What is cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a new form of digital money for the internet age first created after the financial crisis in 2008/2009. Think of it as the currency of the internet. It is a natural evolution of money that enables the freer flow of money around the world. Cryptocurrency does not recognise national borders and is more frictionless than cash money. Benefits of cryptocurrency include speedier transactions of currency and value across the world (send crypto to your crypto address in Italy from Australia within 10 minutes for only a few cents instead of paying transaction fees of 5% plus with traditional payments systems. Speedier transactions and less middlemen taking their cut are some of the attractive benefits of cryptocurrency versus traditional currency. What is block chain technology?

The block chain is a uniquely architected database for digital transactions. To break it down— a block contains a record of new transactions and once that block is full, it is added to a chain of other blocks of transactions; hence the name “block chain”. This information is publicly viewable on an explorer, where eventually each transaction is approved or rejected by a network of computers (called miners). The block chain is considered immutable (meaning unchanging) because in order to alter a block, all previous blocks would have to also be altered. The vast majority of the community would have to agree on the change and be willing to update all subsequent blocks. What is Ethereum ?

Ethereum is an open-source, public, blockchain-based distributed computing platform and operating system featuring smart contract (scripting) functionality. It supports a modified version of Nakamoto consensus via transaction-based state transitions.

What is ether?

Ether is a decentralized digital currency, also known as ETH. In addition to being a tradeable cryptocurrency, ether powers the Ethereum network by paying for transaction fees and computational services. Ether is paving the way for a more intelligent financial platform.

What are ERC20 tokens?
